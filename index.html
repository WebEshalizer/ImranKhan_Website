"Imran Khan: From Cricketing Legend to Political Trailblazer"

Imran Khan, born on October 5, 1952, in Lahore, Pakistan, has led a multifaceted life as a cricketer, politician, and visionary. His early years showcased his exceptional talent as a cricketer, culminating in his captaincy during Pakistan's victorious 1992 Cricket World Cup campaign. Transitioning into politics, Imran Khan founded the Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) party, driven by a vision for a "Naya Pakistan" (New Pakistan) characterized by justice, accountability, and social welfare. As the Prime Minister of Pakistan since 2018, Imran Khan's foreign policy ideology emphasizes regional stability, engagement with China through initiatives like the China-Pakistan Economic Corridor (CPEC), and a pursuit of peace in Afghanistan through Afghan-led and Afghan-owned processes. He has also sought to strengthen ties with the Muslim world, advocating for unity among Muslim nations. Imran Khan's speeches resonate with passion, addressing key issues such as poverty alleviation, education, and healthcare while inspiring unity and progress. His life journey exemplifies determination, leadership, and a relentless pursuit of a prosperous future for Pakistan both domestically and on the global stage.

Imran Khan: From Cricketing Stature to National Icon


Imran Khan's cricketing career is a tale of determination, skill, and ultimate triumph. Khan made his first debut at the age of 16 in Lahore. He played English county cricket from 1971 to 1976 for Worcestershire. During this decade, other teams represented by Khan included Dawood Industries (1975-1976) and Pakistan International Airlines (1975-1976 to 1980-1981). From 1983 to 1988, he played for Sussex.Khan made his Test cricket debut against England in June 1971 at Edgbaston. Three years later, in August 1974, he debuted in the One Day International (ODI) match, once again playing against England at Trent Bridge for the Prudential Trophy. At the height of his career, in 1982, the thirty-year-old Khan took over the captaincy of the Pakistan cricket team from Javed Miandad. Khan led Pakistan to another winning tour in the West Indies, which he has recounted as "the last time I really bowled well". He was declared Man of the Series against West Indies in 1988 when he took 23 wickets in 3 Tests. Khan's career-high as a captain and cricketer came when he led Pakistan to victory in the 1992 Cricket World Cup. Playing with a brittle batting line-up, Khan promoted himself as a batsman to play in the top order along with Javed Miandad, but his contribution as a bowler was minimal. At the age of 39, Khan took the winning last wicket himself.

Imran Khan: Charting a New Course in Pakistani Politics


On 25 April 1996, Khan founded a political party, Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI). Khan announced that he would pull Pakistan out of the U.S. led war on terror and bring peace to the Pashtun tribal belt. Khan led Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf became the opposition party in Punjab and Sindh. Khan became the parliamentary leader of his party. On 31 July 2013 Khan was issued a contempt of court notice for allegedly criticising the superior judiciary. The notice was discharged after Khan submitted before the Supreme Court that he criticised the lower judiciary for their actions during the May 2013 general election while those judicial officers were working as returning officers. PTI-led Khyber Pakhtunkhwa government presented a balanced, tax-free budget for the fiscal year 2013-14. During his provincial government, Khan was criticised for his support for Sami-ul- Haq, the "Father of the Taliban," and giving funds to his seminary, Darul Uloom Haqqania. On 17 August 2018, Khan secured 176 votes and became 22nd Prime Minister of Pakistan and took oath of office on 18 August 2018. Khan ordered top level reshuffling in the country's bureaucracy, including the appointment of Sohail Mahmood as Foreign Secretary, Rizwan Ahmed as Maritime Secretary and Naveed Kamran Baloch as Finance Secretary. His first major appointment in the Pakistan Army was that of Lieutenant General Asim Munir to the key slot of Director-General of Inter-Services Intelligence.
